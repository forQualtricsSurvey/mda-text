* The company will divest its non-energy drink business to TCCC.

* The Coca-Cola Company will become a significant shareholder in the reorganized company.

* Future reporting segments will be restructured to "Concentrate" and "Finished Products".

* New risks related to the TCCC transaction and integration were disclosed.

* The company is terminating distribution agreements with its Anheuser-Busch distributors.

* Price increases are now cited as a driver of revenue growth.

* A caffeine-free version of the flagship energy drink was introduced.

* Stock repurchases were removed from the list of principal uses of cash.

* Operating expenses decreased, reversing the prior year's trend of increasing.

* The discussion on obesity is now focused solely on the energy drink portfolio.

* The Warehouse segment’s sales performance reversed from growth to a decline.

* New professional service costs were incurred for a "TCCC Transaction".

* The Warehouse segment's contribution margin turned from a loss to a profit.

* International operations shifted from reporting an operating loss to a profit.

* The effective tax rate explanation changed, citing profits in foreign jurisdictions.

* A reduction in inventory became a significant source of operating cash flow.

* Cash spent on common stock repurchases was significantly reduced.

* A new disclosure quantifies cash held by the company's foreign subsidiaries.

* The company stated its intention not to repatriate foreign earnings.

* The "Juice Monster™" trademark is added to the list of intangible assets.

* New risks regarding a "TCCC Transaction" and its benefits are introduced.

* The impact of future commercial arrangements with TCCC is now a disclosed risk.

* A new risk is disclosed about TCCC becoming a significant shareholder.

* The sales risk is expanded to include energy drinks acquired from TCCC.

* Guideline compliance risk now refers to industry standards, not company-specific proposals.
